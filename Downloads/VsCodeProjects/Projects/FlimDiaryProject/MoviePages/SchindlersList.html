Schindler's List

Overview

Title: Schindler's List

Director: Steven Spielberg

Producers: Steven Spielberg, Gerald R. Molen, Branko Lustig

Screenplay: Steven Zaillian

Based on: "Schindler's Ark" by Thomas Keneally

Music: John Williams

Cinematography: Janusz Kamiński

Editing: Michael Kahn

Production Companies: Universal Pictures, Amblin Entertainment

Distributor: Universal Pictures

Release Date: December 15, 1993 (United States)

Running Time: 195 minutes

Country: United States

Language: English, Hebrew, German, Polish

Budget: Approximately $22 million

Box Office: $321.2 million

Plot

Schindler's List is based on the true story of Oskar Schindler, a German businessman who saved more than a thousand Polish-Jewish refugees during the Holocaust by employing them in his factories. The film depicts Schindler's gradual transformation from a profit-driven entrepreneur to a compassionate savior, as he risks everything to protect his workers from Nazi persecution.

Main Cast

  • Liam Neeson as Oskar Schindler
  • Ben Kingsley as Itzhak Stern
  • Ralph Fiennes as Amon Göth
  • Caroline Goodall as Emilie Schindler
  • Jonathan Sagall as Poldek Pfefferberg
  • Embeth Davidtz as Helen Hirsch

Production

Development: Steven Spielberg was deeply moved by Thomas Keneally's book "Schindler's Ark" and felt a personal responsibility to bring Oskar Schindler's story to the screen.

Filming Locations: Principal photography took place in Kraków and various locations in Poland.

Black and White: The film is notable for being shot almost entirely in black and white, with only one scene in color.

Reception

Critical Acclaim: Schindler's List received universal acclaim for its poignant storytelling, direction, performances, and emotional impact. It is often regarded as one of the greatest films ever made.

Box Office Success: Despite its difficult subject matter, the film performed well commercially and won seven Academy Awards.

Awards and Nominations

  • Academy Award for Best Picture (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Director - Steven Spielberg (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay - Steven Zaillian (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Cinematography (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Art Direction (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Film Editing (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Original Score - John Williams (Won)
  • Academy Award for Best Sound (Nominated)

Legacy

Impact: Schindler's List is hailed for its educational value and for raising awareness about the Holocaust. It continues to be a powerful reminder of the atrocities committed during World War II.

Recognition: The film's importance in cinema history is underscored by its preservation in the National Film Registry and its inclusion in many lists of the greatest films ever made.

Education: It is often used as a teaching tool in schools and universities to educate students about the Holocaust and the moral choices faced during times of crisis.

Additional Notes

Themes: Schindler's List explores themes of humanity, courage, and the impact of individual actions in the face of overwhelming evil.

Visual Style: Janusz Kamiński's stark cinematography and John Williams' haunting score contribute to the film's emotional depth and historical authenticity.

Legacy of Oskar Schindler: Oskar Schindler was recognized as Righteous Among the Nations for his actions during the Holocaust, saving the lives of many Jews.

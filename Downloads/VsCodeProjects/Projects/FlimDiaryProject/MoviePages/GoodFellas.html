Goodfellas

Overview

Title: Goodfellas

Director: Martin Scorsese

Producers: Irwin Winkler, Barbara De Fina

Screenplay: Nicholas Pileggi, Martin Scorsese

Based on: "Wiseguy" by Nicholas Pileggi

Music: Various Artists

Cinematography: Michael Ballhaus

Editing: Thelma Schoonmaker

Production Companies: Warner Bros., Village Roadshow Pictures

Distributor: Warner Bros.

Release Date: September 19, 1990 (United States)

Running Time: 146 minutes

Country: United States

Language: English

Budget: Approximately $25 million

Box Office: Grossed over $46 million worldwide

Plot

"Goodfellas" follows the rise and fall of mob associate Henry Hill, spanning his life from the 1950s to the 1980s. It explores his involvement in the Lucchese crime family and his relationships with fellow mobsters Jimmy Conway and Tommy DeVito, as well as his struggles with addiction and betrayal.

Main Cast

  • Robert De Niro as James "Jimmy" Conway
  • Ray Liotta as Henry Hill
  • Joe Pesci as Tommy DeVito
  • Lorraine Bracco as Karen Hill
  • Paul Sorvino as Paul Cicero
  • Frank Sivero as Frankie Carbone
  • Mike Starr as Frenchy

Production

Development: Martin Scorsese adapted the screenplay from Nicholas Pileggi's book "Wiseguy," focusing on authenticity and realism in depicting organized crime.

Filming Locations: Principal photography took place in New York City and surrounding areas, capturing the authentic settings of Henry Hill's life.

Soundtrack: The film's soundtrack features a mix of classic rock, pop, and jazz tracks from the era, enhancing its period authenticity.

Reception

Critical Acclaim: "Goodfellas" received widespread critical acclaim for its direction, performances, and screenplay, earning praise for its portrayal of organized crime and its impact on American cinema.

Box Office Success: It grossed over $46 million worldwide, becoming a profitable and influential film in the crime genre.

Awards and Nominations

  • Academy Awards for Best Supporting Actor (Joe Pesci), Best Film Editing (Thelma Schoonmaker), and Best Adapted Screenplay (Nicholas Pileggi, Martin Scorsese) (Won)
  • Nominated for several other Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director, recognizing its achievements in filmmaking.

Legacy

Cultural Impact: "Goodfellas" remains a seminal work in the crime genre, influencing subsequent films and television series with its portrayal of mafia life and its narrative structure.

Style and Influence: Known for its use of long tracking shots, voice-over narration, and eclectic soundtrack, the film has left a lasting impact on filmmaking techniques.

Popularity: Continues to be celebrated for its quotable dialogue, memorable scenes, and the authenticity of its portrayal of organized crime.

Additional Notes

Themes: Explores themes of loyalty, betrayal, ambition, and the consequences of a life of crime, offering a nuanced portrayal of its characters' motivations and actions.

Cinematography: Michael Ballhaus's cinematography captures the gritty realism of urban life and the opulence of the mob world, enhancing the film's atmosphere and storytelling.

Sound Design: The film's sound design, including dialogue delivery and ambient noise, contributes to its immersive portrayal of the underworld.

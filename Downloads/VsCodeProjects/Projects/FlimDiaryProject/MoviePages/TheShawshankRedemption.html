The Shawshank Redemption

Overview

Title: The Shawshank Redemption

Director: Frank Darabont

Screenplay: Frank Darabont (based on Stephen King's novella)

Music: Thomas Newman

Cinematography: Roger Deakins

Editing: Richard Francis-Bruce

Production Companies: Castle Rock Entertainment, Columbia Pictures

Distributor: Columbia Pictures

Release Date: September 23, 1994 (USA)

Running Time: 142 minutes

Country: United States

Language: English

Duration: 2 hours 22 minutes

Plot

Banker Andy Dufresne is convicted of a double murder he did not commit and is sentenced to life in Shawshank State Penitentiary. Over the years, he befriends fellow inmate Ellis "Red" Redding, adapts to life in prison, and uses his banking skills to win favor with the warden and fellow prisoners.

Main Cast

  • Tim Robbins as Andy Dufresne
  • Morgan Freeman as Ellis "Red" Redding
  • Bob Gunton as Warden Samuel Norton
  • William Sadler as Heywood
  • Clancy Brown as Captain Byron Hadley
  • Gil Bellows as Tommy Williams
  • James Whitmore as Brooks Hatlen

Production

"The Shawshank Redemption" was adapted from Stephen King's novella "Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption." Directed by Frank Darabont, the film was primarily shot at the Ohio State Reformatory in Mansfield, Ohio, with additional scenes filmed in nearby locations.

Reception

"The Shawshank Redemption" received positive reviews from critics but initially struggled at the box office. Over time, it gained a strong following through word of mouth and became a beloved classic. It is now widely regarded as one of the greatest films of all time.

The film earned seven Academy Award nominations, including Best Picture and Best Actor for Morgan Freeman.

Awards and Nominations

  • Academy Award for Best Picture - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role (Morgan Freeman) - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Cinematography - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Sound Mixing - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Film Editing - Nominated
  • Academy Award for Best Original Score - Nominated

Legacy

"The Shawshank Redemption" has left a lasting impact on popular culture, inspiring audiences with its themes of hope, friendship, and the resilience of the human spirit. It continues to be celebrated for its powerful storytelling and memorable performances.

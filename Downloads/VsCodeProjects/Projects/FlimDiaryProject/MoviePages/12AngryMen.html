12 Angry Men

Overview

Title: 12 Angry Men

Director: Sidney Lumet

Producer: Henry Fonda, Reginald Rose

Screenplay: Reginald Rose

Based on: "Twelve Angry Men" by Reginald Rose

Music: Kenyon Hopkins

Cinematography: Boris Kaufman

Editing: Carl Lerner

Production Company: Orion-Nova Productions

Distributor: United Artists

Release Date: April 10, 1957 (USA)

Running Time: 96 minutes

Country: United States

Language: English

Box Office: $2 million (estimated)

Plot

In "12 Angry Men," a jury deliberates over the trial of a young man accused of murder. Initially, all but one of the jurors are convinced of his guilt, but as they discuss the evidence, doubts emerge, revealing personal biases and raising questions about justice and the legal system.

Production

Development: The film adaptation of "Twelve Angry Men," originally a teleplay, was directed by Sidney Lumet and produced by Henry Fonda, who also starred in the film.

Filming: Most of the movie was filmed in a single room, emphasizing the intense, claustrophobic atmosphere of the jury room.

Black and White: The film is shot entirely in black and white, contributing to its dramatic and stark presentation.

Reception

Critical Acclaim: "12 Angry Men" received critical acclaim for its powerful performances, gripping screenplay, and insightful commentary on the legal system and human nature.

Legacy: The film is regarded as a classic of American cinema and is often studied for its depiction of jury dynamics and moral dilemmas.

Awards and Nominations

  • Nominated for 3 Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director (Sidney Lumet)
  • Inducted into the National Film Registry in 2007 for its cultural, historical, and aesthetic significance

Legacy

Influence: "12 Angry Men" is celebrated for its exploration of themes such as justice, prejudice, and the power of persuasion. It remains a benchmark for courtroom dramas and ensemble acting.

Adaptations: The story has been adapted into various formats, including stage plays and television productions, testament to its enduring appeal.

Remakes: The film has inspired several international remakes, showcasing its universal themes and narrative resonance.

Additional Notes

Themes: The film explores themes of justice, prejudice, reasonable doubt, and the responsibility of jurors in the legal process.

Impact: Its minimalist setting and intense dialogue-driven narrative have influenced many filmmakers and storytellers.
